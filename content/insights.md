Technology decisions shape how institutions govern, preserve knowledge, manage risk and serve people. Skymouse Insights examines the practical questions behind digital transformation, governance technology, open source and institutional capability.

## Initial article briefs

### Article 1: Governance needs a system of record

Boards produce more than meeting minutes. They produce decisions, obligations, risks, approvals and institutional history. This article should explain why governance information cannot remain scattered across email, shared drives, printed packs and individual memory.

Suggested conclusion: good governance is not only about making the right decision; it is also about ensuring the institution can show what was decided, why it was decided and what happened next.

### Article 2: Open source is not free software—it is strategic freedom

Explain that the value of open source is not merely avoiding licence fees. Its deeper value can include portability, transparency, local capability, integration freedom and reduced dependence. Also acknowledge the responsibilities: security, hosting, maintenance, governance and support.

Suggested conclusion: independence does not mean operating without partners; it means choosing partners without surrendering control.

### Article 3: The website is no longer the digital transformation

Many organisations still treat a new website as evidence of digital transformation. Explain the difference between a public-facing redesign and the deeper work of improving workflows, data, services, controls and decision-making.

Suggested conclusion: a modern homepage can improve perception, but only a better operating system improves the institution.

### Article 4: Stop replacing systems that still work

Discuss the tendency to prescribe complete replacement before understanding a useful legacy system. Use the principle of wrapping, integrating and improving where appropriate.

Suggested conclusion: modernisation is not measured by how much old technology was discarded, but by how much institutional performance improved.

### Article 5: African technology companies must build leverage

Explore why custom software firms remain trapped when every project begins from zero. Discuss reusable components, products, domain expertise, managed services, intellectual property and distribution.

Suggested conclusion: the company that only sells hours must start the race again every Monday; the company that builds leverage carries last week’s progress forward.

## Publishing standard

Every article should:

- Begin with a clear institutional problem or provocative observation
- Use practical examples rather than empty technology language
- Be understandable to executives, governance professionals and technical readers
- Avoid exaggerated claims
- End with an intelligent conclusion or practical principle
- Connect naturally to a Skymouse capability without becoming a sales brochure
